Team Australia Shines at Special Olympics World Winter Games in Turin

Team Australia is returning home after a successful performance at the Special Olympics World Winter Games in Turin, Italy, bringing home a total of 15 medals, including three gold, five silver, and seven bronze.

As the world celebrates International Women’s Day on 8 March, Georgina Frost, Head of Delegation for Team Australia, is gearing up to lead an inspiring group of athletes to the Special Olympics World Winter Games in Turin, Italy.

Participate

If you have intellectual disability and/or autism, you can enjoy sport at Special Olympics Australia. If you want to do good and be inspired, you can volunteer. If you love to coach or officiate, you’re welcome here.

Athletes

If you have intellectual disability/autism, come and join Special Olympics Australia. We can help you get fit, have fun, build confidence and make friends. Regardless of your age, ability or aspirations, this is your place to belong.

Volunteers

Join Special Olympics Australia to be part of a supportive sporting community where athletes with intellectual disability/autism consistently inspire joy.

Coaches and Officials

Join Special Olympics Australia to bring quality instruction and support to people with intellectual disability/autism so they can enjoy sport and community. If you need to learn how, or upskill, our Inclusive Sport Academy has you covered.

Become a Coach or Official

Inclusive Sport Academy

If you want to polish your skills to deliver sport and physical activity programs to people with intellectual disability and/or autism you should enrol in our Inclusive Sport Academy. Through this platform we share our sports inclusion expertise for the benefit of teachers, coaches, tertiary students, allied health professionals and inclusion advocates.
