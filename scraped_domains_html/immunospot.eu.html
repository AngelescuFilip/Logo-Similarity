Dual-Color Enzymatic vs. FluoroSpot analysis
In general, relying upon enzymatic amplification, ELISPOT assays are more sensitive, detecting low per-cell analyte secretion by individual cells compared to FluoroSpot detection, as the latter does not involve enzymatic amplification of the signal. When the secreted analyte is abundant, however, both methods are equally suited to detect and enumerate secretory foot prints.

ELISPOT assays can be performed in a dual color format that, when optimized, is suited for detection of analyte co-expression by individual antigen-specific T cells with equal sensitivity to FluoroSpot or intracytoplasmic staining by flow cytometry (Ref). Dual-Color Enzymatic assays have since been optimized by CTL (Ref) by (a) introducing true-blue and true-red substrates that enable clear color distinction via RGB machine reading, (b) modifications of the protocol such that both substrates are transparent, that is, neither overdevelop nor cover up/mask spots of the other color, (c) introducing backlight well illumination for all CTL Reader models that further increases the color transparency of spots, and (d) having developed a dedicated Dual-Color ELISPOT analysis software that is installed on every CTL Reader. Thus, even if you have access to a fluorescence-capable reader, CTLs optimized Dual-Color Enzymatic kits are a valid and cost-efficient alternative to two-color FluoroSpot analysis. 384-well vs. 96-well ELISPOT/FluoroSpot analysis
ELISPOT assays require special plates with PVDF membrane bottoms to enable optimal coating with capture antibodies or antigens. CTL offers ELISPOT kits in both 96-well and 384-well format. The membrane surface area of a well in a 384-well plate is exactly one third of that in a 96-well plate (not one fourth, as one might expect). Subsequently, ELISPOT assays in 384-well format permit a 1:3 miniaturization of the standard 96-well assay with only one third of PBMC needed, but the spot counts per well will also be precisely one third per well in the miniaturized assay (Ref). Thus, on the upside, 384-well assays permit saving two third of the valuable cells, while still clearly revealing relatively high and mid-ranged frequency spot forming units (SFU) in a 1:3 reduced scale/well. On the downside, low frequency SFU counts which might still be highly significant in 96 -well format will also be reduced 1:3, possibly resulting in the loss of detecting weak responses in 384-well format. Also, as the SFU counts per well drop, the Poisson noise increases, resulting in looser replicates in 384-well format. The reduced cell utilization in the 384-well format therefore results in a compensatory reduction in the limit of detection. When choosing between the two well formats, you will need to decide which is more important for your test: saving cells, or detecting the weakest of responses.

All CTL Reader models are capable of 384- and 96-well ELISPOT analysis.

Note: low autofluorescence PVDF membrane plates are not available in 384 well format. Thus, 384well miniaturization is only feasible in single- and double-color ELISPOT, but not with FluoroSpot.

Reagent Tracker Suite

The Reagent Tracker Suite provides audit trails for the accurate implementation of ELISPOT/FlouroSpot assays, revealing pipetting errors made (https://doi.org/10.3390/cells7010003). Biologically neutral dyes are used to colour different antigens and the Reagent Tracker Software checks whether in each assigned well the right antigen/colour has indeed been plated (revealing qualitative pipetting errors), and whether the antigen pipetting volume was correct (revealing quantitative pipetting errors). The Reagent Tracker Suite generates automatic printable reports and is also ideally suited for proficiency testing of lab personnel, and for training of novices in the lab.

CTL has been committed to elevating ELISPOT/FluoroSpot assays so they can meet regulatory requirements for transparency and audit trails. The Reagent Tracker Suite is one of the important steps in that direction.
